Prologue: On Structure and the Absence of Certainty

This book was written in Berlin, in sections, over several years. It began as a private document — not a theoretical claim but a personal reckoning. The theology came later. The framework arrived after the experience, not before it.

What follows is structured — deliberately, carefully — but structure is not the same as certainty. The architecture exists to carry something. It is not the thing itself.

The reader who wants a manual will find partial satisfaction here. The reader who wants a philosophy will find more. The reader who wants to understand what it means to inhabit a specific orientation — to follow it past the point where most frameworks stop — will find what they came for.

The text does not argue that its claims are universal. It argues that they are precise. Precision is worth more than universality when the subject is desire.
