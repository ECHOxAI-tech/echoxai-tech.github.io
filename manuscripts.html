01 — Literary Thriller

The Alexander Cipher

A novel — Eko Svenningsson

At 8:56 PM on Thursday, October 30, 2025, the Museum kept different hours than the world outside. Official closing was six. After that, the building continued on its own terms — security lights, climate systems, soft relays clicking in the walls. A controlled afterlife. Emma Voss knew these hours best. The hours with fewer voices. Fewer faces. Fewer reasons to explain herself.

Twins separated at birth, engineered into forbidden intimacy by a centuries-old harvesting system. A museum conservator. A set of paired names repeated across two millennia of recorded history. A trust that has been moving money — and people — since 1893. Literary thriller. New York, present day. The mechanism inside words.

02 — Philosophy / Theology

The Dark Hierarchy

A Treatise on Authority, Initiation, and the Order — Echo Kronborg

This book was written in Berlin, in sections, over several years. It began as a private document — not a theoretical claim but a personal reckoning. The theology came later. The framework arrived after the experience, not before it. What follows is structured — deliberately, carefully — but structure is not the same as certainty. This book is not a confession. It is version control.

A philosophical and theological treatise on desire, authority, and structured intimacy — followed past the point where most frameworks stop. Written under the construct Echo Kronborg. In the tradition of Bataille, Sacher-Masoch, and Nin: the fictional author as the distance in which truth can be spoken.

04 — Poetry Collection

The Book of Names

A completed poetry collection — Eko Svenningsson

Every name is an act of preservation. To name is to insist on existence — to refuse disappearance. The Book of Names gathers voices inward, writing people into permanence through Berlin's geography, its light, its irreversible moments.

A completed collection exploring witnessing, naming as sacred preservation, and urban mysticism through Berlin's geography. Structured around three mythological artifacts: Water (baptism / transformation), Mirror (reflection / witness), Pen (inscription / creation).

Suitable for submission to Copper Canyon, Nightboat, or Graywolf. Publication-ready.
